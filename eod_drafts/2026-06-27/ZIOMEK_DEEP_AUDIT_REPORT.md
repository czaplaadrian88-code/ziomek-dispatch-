# ZIOMEK — DEEP AUDIT REPORT (2026-06-27, read-only)

## 0. Executive summary

**Overall health: GOOD.** No P0 found. No finding survived verification at P1 — both P1 candidates were downgraded to P2 on close reading. The engine's HARD-before-SOFT discipline holds at runtime (`_assert_feasibility_first`), the tier-aware R6 cap (35 T1/2 / 40 T3) is correctly implemented, always-propose/best-effort sentinels are correct-by-design, and the large majority of "obvious bug / dead code" claims collapsed on verification (10 findings fully refuted). The dominant risk class is **latent-and-dormant**: most real defects sit behind OFF flags (O2 sweep, post-shift penalty, LGBM-primary, chain-eta, prep-bias-table) or behind the intentionally-muted Telegram/postpone subsystem, and are therefore pre-flip / pre-reenable hazards rather than live incidents.

**What is healthy:**
- Feasibility-first is enforced; the one documented post-guard mutation (feas-carry-readmit) is ACK'd, replay-validated, marker-serialized, and only ever *lowers* R6 breach.
- R6 thermal cap, paczka exemption, equal-treatment buckets are wired in the live decision path.
- `AUTO_ASSIGN=0` + Telegram muted → all proposals are human-reviewed in the coordinator console, shrinking blast radius of every selection-layer issue.

**Top risks (plain language):**
1. **App vs console route divergence is LIVE** (`cap-carried-relax-app-console-divergence`, P2): the courier app tells drivers to deliver cooling food *first*, contradicting the console and the engine's carried-first-relax optimization, on 44–75 bags/day. Real operational + execution-order impact today.
2. **Equal-treatment is silently undone for 20% of orders** (`twin-pln-pure-resort-stale-bucket`, P2, upgraded from P3): the PLN A/B resort arm is LIVE (`ENABLE_E2_PLN_AB=1` in-process) but uses a pre-equal-treatment inline bucket, demoting no_gps/pre_shift candidates the main path treats equally.
3. **OSRM fallback double-counts traffic** (`osrm-fallback-double-traffic`, P2): during an OSRM outage at peak, drive times inflate ~1.5×, manufacturing spurious R6 breaches exactly when the system is already degraded. Latent (fallback ~0 when healthy) but amplifies harm at the worst moment.
4. **Same-restaurant grouping double-inserts the super-pickup** in greedy/bruteforce (`rst-grouping-greedy-double-pickup`, P2): masked by OR-Tools today, but the documented 1-flag emergency rollback (`ENABLE_V326_OR_TOOLS_TSP=False`) un-masks it broadly during an incident.
5. **Test isolation leaks production flag values** (`tests-etap4-registry-drift-isolation-leak`, P2, downgraded from P1): ~24 live flags missing from `ETAP4_DECISION_FLAGS`; tests intending to assert OFF/baseline behavior silently run ON. A demonstrated failing OFF-baseline test proves the leak.
6. **A live R6 HARD-path flag has zero ON≠OFF test** (`feas-r6-bagcap-untested-live`, P2, downgraded from P1): `ENABLE_ETA_QUANTILE_R6_BAGCAP` gates whether a gold bag passes the hardest constraint, untested.
7. **Pre-flip validation gates are broken by un-serialized metrics** (`metser-post-shift-overrun-not-serialized`, P2): the metric that would lead best_effort selection on flip never reaches `shadow_decisions.jsonl`, so the protocol's ETAP-5 "prove positive impact" step has no data source.
8. **The postpone subsystem is quietly broken** (`dsi-postpone-sweeper-orders-state-schema-mismatch` / `czas-postpone-*`): schema mismatch (`cid` vs `courier_id`, `orders` nesting) makes resolution-detection and re-emit dead; dormant only because Telegram is muted, but activates the moment postpone is re-enabled.

A recurring meta-theme: **flag-flips and service-re-enables are NOT neutral.** Several dormant defects are armed precisely by the rollback/re-enable lever a future operator is most likely to pull under stress (OR-Tools rollback, Telegram re-enable, O2 02.07 flip).
